Your complete guide to using AlfredHelps

How Alfred Works

Think of Alfred as a smart assistant with a toolbox. When you ask for something, Alfred's AI brain figures out the best tool for the job. It can search the web for the latest news, generate an image from your imagination, or just have a friendly chat. It learns from your conversations to give you a more personalized experience over time.

Getting Started in 3 Easy Steps

1. Add Alfred to Your Server

Click the "Add to Discord" button on our website or in Alfred's profile. You'll be asked to authorize it to join your server.

2. Choose Your AI's Vibe

The first time you talk to Alfred, it will ask you to choose a personality: a more formal, accurate AI or a more casual, fun one. You can change this anytime in /user-settings.

3. Start Chatting!

There are a few ways to talk to Alfred:

  • Mention: Simply @AlfredHelps in any channel.
  • Trigger Word: Just saying "alfred" in a message will get its attention (server admins can change this).
  • Slash Commands: Use commands like /ask for direct questions. This is the best way to use Alfred in DMs.
  • Sessions: Use /session-start to have Alfred reply to every message in a channel without needing to be mentioned. Perfect for brainstorming or Q&A!

Fun Things You Can Do

AI Conversation & Personalization

  • Pick Your AI: Use /user-settings to switch between AI personalities. The "Default" model is great for general use, while the "Fun Learning Buddy" offers a more humorous experience. Premium subscribers get access to our most powerful and creative models.
  • Set the Mood: In /user-settings, switch between "Casual Mode" for creative chats and "Serious Mode" for professional and precise answers.
  • Create Your Own AI (Premium): Subscribers can use /create-personality to build their own AI assistants with unique names, instructions, and behaviors.

Image Magic

  • Generate Images: Use /imagine with a descriptive prompt to create AI-generated images in various styles.
  • Reimagine Existing Images: Right-click on any image and select `Apps > Reimagine`. Alfred will create a new image inspired by the original.
  • Describe What You See: Right-click an image and use `Apps > Describe` to get a detailed text description of the picture.

Powerful Tools

  • Deep Research: Use /deep-research [topic] for a detailed report on any subject. Alfred will browse the web and summarize its findings for you.
  • Summarize Anything: Right-click on a long message or an image and use `Apps > Summarize` to get a quick summary.

Your Data, Your Control

You have full control over your data.

  • Personalize Your Experience: The /user-settings command is your hub for personalization. You can also go to `Privacy Settings` to turn off message analysis, which helps Alfred learn your style.
  • Delete Your History: Use /delete-history to clear Alfred's memory in the current channel. Use /delete-all-history to permanently erase all of your stored messages everywhere.

For Server Admins

Admins with "Manage Server" permissions can use /server-settings to customize Alfred:

  • Family Friendly Mode: Keep conversations clean by restricting the bot to a family-friendly AI.
  • Custom Trigger Word: Change the word that activates Alfred (the default is "alfred").
  • General Chatter: Allow Alfred to occasionally join in on conversations to make the server more lively.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

Having trouble? Here are some common questions and answers.

  • Why isn't Alfred responding?
    • Make sure Alfred has permission to read and send messages in the channel.
    • Check the server's trigger word in /server-settings.
    • You might be on a cooldown for sending messages too quickly.
  • Why did I get an "interaction failed" error? This usually means Alfred is thinking and took a little too long to respond. Try your request again in a few moments.
  • How do I use the right-click commands (like Summarize or Reimagine)? On desktop, right-click a message or image. On mobile, tap and hold the message, then find the "Apps" option in the menu that appears.
